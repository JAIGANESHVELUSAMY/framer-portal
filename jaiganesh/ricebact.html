Rice Bacterial Leaf Blight

  1. Observed 1-3 weeks after transplanting
  2. Green water-soaked layer along the cut portion or leaf tip of leaves as early symptom
  3. Leaves wilt and roll up and become grayish green to yellow
  4. Entire plant wilt completely
  1. To distinguish kresek symptoms from stem borer damage, the lower end of the infected seedling can be squeezed between the fingers.
  2. Yellowish bacterial ooze may be seen coming out of the cut ends.
  3. The cut portion can be observed against the light to see the bacterial ooze streaming out from the cut ends into the water
  4. The cut portion can be observed against the light to see the bacterial ooze streaming out from the cut ends into the water

DIAGONISATION

  1. Seed treatment with bleaching powder (100g/l) and zinc sulfate (2%) reduce bacterial blight.
  2. Seed treatment - seed soaking for 8 hours in Agrimycin (0.025%) and wettable ceresan (0.05%) followed by hot water treatment for 30 min at 52-54oC;
  3. seed soaking for 8 hours in ceresan (0.1%) and treat with Streptocyclin (3g in 1 litre);
  4. Spray Streptomycin sulphate + Tetracycline combination 300 g + Copper oxychloride 1.25kg/ha. If necessary repeat 15 days later.
  5. Application of bleaching powder @ 5 kg/ha in the irrigation water is recommended in the kresek stage.
  6. Foliar spray with copper fungicides alternatively with Strepto-cyclin (250 ppm) to check secondary spread.
